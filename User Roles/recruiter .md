# 4. Recruiter

## 4.1 Role Definition
The Recruiter is a specialized HR professional dedicated to talent acquisition, either as an in-house recruiter for a company or as part of an external recruitment agency. They focus on sourcing, screening, and managing high volumes of candidates, often working on multiple positions simultaneously across different departments.

## 4.2 User Profile Characteristics

### Demographics
- **Role:** In-house Recruiter, Technical Recruiter, Executive Recruiter, Recruitment Coordinator  
- **Employment Type:** Full-time employee or contract/agency recruiter  
- **Age Range:** 25-45 years old  
- **Education:** Bachelor's degree in HR, Business, Psychology, or related field  
- **Certifications:** PHR, SHRM-CP, or recruitment-specific certifications  
- **Technical Proficiency:** High; expert users of ATS and recruitment tools  
- **Daily Workload:** Managing 10-30 active job openings simultaneously  

### Behavioral Traits
- High-volume processors with systematic approaches  
- Relationship builders with candidates and hiring managers  
- Metrics-driven and performance-oriented  
- Multi-tasking experts juggling multiple searches  
- Proactive sourcers always building talent pipelines  
- Communication specialists across all channels  
- Resilient in face of rejection and ghosting  
- Competitive about placement success rates  

### Persona Examples

**Sarah - In-House Technical Recruiter**
- 29 years old, 5 years recruiting experience  
- Specializes in engineering and product roles  
- Manages 15-20 open positions  
- Works closely with 8 hiring managers  
- Expert in tech sourcing (LinkedIn, GitHub, Stack Overflow)  
- Measured on time-to-fill and quality of hire  
- Uses Boolean search strings and sourcing automation  

**James - Agency Recruiter**
- 32 years old, 7 years agency experience  
- Works with 10-15 client companies  
- Focuses on executive and senior-level placements  
- Measured on commission and placements  
- Extensive professional network  
- Expert negotiator and relationship manager  
- Manages candidate pipelines across multiple opportunities  

## 4.3 Core Objectives & Goals

### Primary Goals
- **Fill Positions Rapidly:** Achieve aggressive time-to-fill targets (typically 20-30 days)  
- **Maintain Quality:** Present only qualified candidates who meet hiring manager criteria  
- **Build Talent Pipelines:** Continuously source and engage passive candidates  
- **Candidate Experience:** Create positive experiences that enhance employer brand  
- **Process Efficiency:** Optimize workflows to handle high application volumes  
- **Stakeholder Management:** Keep hiring managers informed and engaged  

### Secondary Goals
- Expand professional network and candidate database  
- Develop deep understanding of roles and technical requirements  
- Improve sourcing techniques and channels  
- Increase offer acceptance rates  
- Build strong relationships with hiring managers  
- Stay current on market trends and compensation  
- Develop expertise in specific industries or roles  

### Performance Targets (Typical)
- **Time-to-fill:** 25 days average  
- **Submissions-to-interview ratio:** 3:1 or better  
- **Interview-to-offer ratio:** 3:1 or better  
- **Offer acceptance rate:** 85%+  
- **Hiring manager satisfaction:** 4.5/5  
- **Monthly placements:** 2-4 (depending on role complexity)  
- **Pipeline size:** 50+ engaged candidates per open role  

## 4.4 Pain Points & Challenges

### Volume & Workload Challenges
- **Application Overload:** Processing 50-100+ applications per position  
- **Multi-Position Juggling:** Managing 15-25 open positions simultaneously  
- **Administrative Burden:** Data entry, status updates, and documentation consume 30-40% of time  
- **Context Switching:** Constantly shifting between different roles, requirements, and stakeholders  
- **Urgent Requests:** Hiring managers wanting immediate action on all positions  

### Sourcing Challenges
- **Passive Candidate Engagement:** Difficulty reaching candidates who aren't actively looking  
- **Talent Shortages:** Not enough qualified candidates for specialized roles  
- **Market Competition:** Other companies recruiting same candidate pool  
- **Sourcing Tool Limitations:** Need to use multiple platforms (LinkedIn, GitHub, job boards)  
- **Response Rates:** Low reply rates to outreach messages (typically 5-15%)  

### Process Challenges
- **Hiring Manager Alignment:** Vague or changing role requirements  
- **Slow Decision-Making:** Feedback delays cause candidate drop-off  
- **Interview Scheduling:** Coordinating calendars across multiple participants  
- **Offer Delays:** Legal, finance, or executive approval slows offers  
- **System Fragmentation:** Using multiple disconnected tools (ATS, email, calendar, LinkedIn)  

### Communication Challenges
- **Candidate Ghosting:** Candidates stop responding suddenly  
- **Interview No-Shows:** Last-minute cancellations impact schedules  
- **Offer Declines:** Candidates accepting counteroffers or other opportunities  
- **High-Touch Balance:** Maintaining personal connection at scale  
- **Multi-Channel Management:** Tracking communications across email, phone, text, LinkedIn  

### Strategic Challenges
- **Employer Brand:** Company not well-known or has reputation challenges  
- **Compensation Gaps:** Budget constraints limit candidate pool  
- **Remote Work Expectations:** Candidates want flexibility company won't provide  
- **Diversity Goals:** Building diverse pipelines requires intentional effort  
- **Market Intelligence:** Keeping current on salary trends and competition

  - Source effectiveness  
  - Pipeline velocity

#### Administrative Functions
- Maintain accurate candidate records  
- Document all interactions and decisions  
- Ensure compliance with hiring policies  
- Update candidate statuses promptly  
- Archive completed searches  
- Maintain data quality in system  
- Generate offer letters (with approval)  
- Track onboarding status for placed candidates  

### Restrictions
- Cannot access billing or subscription settings  
- Cannot modify company profile (unless also admin)  
- Cannot delete or permanently remove candidate data  
- Cannot bypass hiring manager approval for offers  
- Limited access to company-wide financial data  
- Cannot modify system-wide settings or workflows  
- May have read-only access to other recruiters' candidates (depending on company settings)  

# 4.6 User Journey & Workflows

## Daily Workflow (Typical Recruiter Day)

### Morning (8:00 AM - 12:00 PM)
**Dashboard Review (8:00-8:30 AM)**
- Check overnight applications (international candidates)
- Review notification summary
- Prioritize urgent positions and hot candidates
- Check hiring manager requests
- Review day's interview schedule
- Set daily goals and priorities

**Application Screening (8:30-10:30 AM - Peak Productivity Time)**
- Review new applications by priority position
- Rapid screening: 2-3 minutes per application
- Qualification verification against job requirements
- Screen for red flags or standout achievements
- Bulk reject clearly unqualified (5-10 at a time)
- Move qualified to "Phone Screen" status
- Add quick notes on each reviewed candidate

**Phone Screens (10:30-12:00 PM)**
- Conduct 4-6 phone screens (15-20 minutes each)
- Use standardized screening script
- Assess basic qualifications and interest level
- Gauge salary expectations
- Explain process and timeline
- Collect additional information
- Add detailed screening notes immediately after
- Update candidate status based on outcome

### Afternoon (1:00 PM - 5:00 PM)
**Active Sourcing (1:00-2:30 PM)**
- LinkedIn searches for passive candidates
- Review employee referrals
- Search internal candidate database for matches
- Send personalized outreach messages (15-20 per day)
- Engage with responded candidates
- Add new candidates to talent pools
- Tag candidates for specific opportunities

**Hiring Manager Sync (2:30-3:30 PM)**
- Present shortlisted candidates
- Discuss feedback on previous submissions
- Clarify requirements for challenging searches
- Coordinate interview schedules
- Update on search progress
- Discuss market conditions and challenges
- Align on next steps and priorities

**Interview Coordination (3:30-4:30 PM)**
- Schedule upcoming interviews
- Send confirmations and calendar invitations
- Prepare interview packets for hiring teams
- Confirm candidate attendance
- Send reminders for next-day interviews
- Reschedule as needed
- Update interview tracking spreadsheet

**Administrative & Follow-Up (4:30-5:00 PM)**
- Update all candidate statuses
- Send status update emails to waiting candidates
- Log all activities and interactions
- Prepare offer letters (if approved)
- Follow up on pending feedback
- Clean up notes and documentation
- Plan next day's activities

## Weekly Recurring Activities
**Monday:**
- Review week's priorities with HR lead
- Set weekly goals (screenings, interviews, offers)
- Check on urgent positions
- Plan sourcing strategy for hard-to-fill roles

**Tuesday-Thursday:**
- Core recruiting activities (as daily workflow above)
- Attend standing hiring team meetings

- Network and build relationships

**Friday:**
- Week-in-review: metrics and achievements
- Prepare weekend pipeline report
- Send update emails to hiring managers
- Follow up on outstanding items
- Plan next week's priorities

- Pipeline cleanup and organization

## Key Workflow Scenarios

### Scenario 1: New Job Requisition Intake
**Kickoff Meeting (30-45 minutes)**
- Meet with hiring manager to understand role
- Review job description thoroughly
- Clarify must-have vs. nice-to-have qualifications
- Discuss ideal candidate profile
- Understand team dynamics and culture fit
- Confirm salary range and budget
- Agree on timeline and process
- Set communication cadence

**Job Posting Optimization (1-2 hours)**
- Research market for similar roles
- Identify appropriate job title and keywords
- Write compelling job description
- Add relevant screening questions
- Set up automated responses
- Determine job posting channels
- Get hiring manager approval
- Publish and promote posting

**Initial Sourcing Sprint (First 3 days)**
- Search internal candidate database
- Conduct LinkedIn sourcing
- Review employee referrals
- Post to specialized job boards
- Share with recruitment networks
- Reach out to 20-30 passive candidates
- Build initial pipeline of 15-20 candidates

**First Submission Package (End of Week 1)**
- Screen top 10-15 applicants
- Conduct phone screens with 5-8 qualified candidates
- Prepare candidate summary packets
- Present 3-5 strong candidates to hiring manager
- Coordinate first round interviews
- Continue sourcing for backup pipeline

### Scenario 2: High-Volume Position (100+ Applications)
**Screening Strategy**
- Set up advanced filters for minimum qualifications
- Use screening questions to auto-reject
- Batch review applications by qualification level
- Focus on top 20% of applicants first
- Use keyword search for must-have skills
- Create comparison views for similar candidates

**Efficient Processing**
- Allocate 2-hour blocks for focused screening
- Use templates for common communications
- Bulk status updates and rejections
- Delegate initial screening to coordinator (if available)
- Focus hiring manager on top 10-15 candidates only
- Maintain "maybe" pool for reconsideration if needed

**Quality Control**
- Don't sacrifice quality for speed
- Use structured scorecards for consistency
- Double-check top candidates thoroughly
- Get hiring manager input on borderline candidates
- Track quality metrics (interview-to-offer ratio)

### Scenario 3: Hard-to-Fill Specialized Role
**Market Research (2-3 hours)**
- Research market availability
- Identify companies with target talent
- Understand competitive compensation
- Map out candidate sources
- Identify niche job boards or communities

**Proactive Sourcing Focus (Ongoing)**
- Spend 60% time on active sourcing
- Personalized outreach to passive candidates
- Leverage employee networks for referrals
- Attend industry events or meetups
- Engage in technical communities (GitHub, Stack Overflow)
- Consider contractors or consultants

**Flexibility & Creativity**
- Discuss requirement flexibility with hiring manager
- Consider trainable candidates with adjacent skills
- Look at career changers or bootcamp graduates
- Expand geographic search to remote candidates
- Review compensation against market data
- Create compelling value proposition beyond salary

### Scenario 4: Executive/Senior-Level Search
**Confidential Search Management**
- Maintain discretion about company and role
- Use blind job postings if necessary
- Personal outreach to senior-level network
- Engage executive recruiters if needed
- Direct sourcing from target companies

**White-Glove Candidate Experience**
- High-touch, personalized communication
- Flexibility with interview scheduling
- Executive-level interview panel
- Comprehensive assessment process
- Detailed offer negotiations
- Maintain relationship until start date and beyond

**Stakeholder Management**
- Coordinate with C-level executives
- Manage expectations on timeline (typically longer)
- Present comprehensive candidate packages
- Facilitate multiple stakeholder interviews
- Navigate complex approval processes

### Scenario 5: Candidate Drop-Off or Ghosting
**Immediate Response**
- Attempt contact via multiple channels (email, phone, text)
- Send friendly check-in message
- Provide flexibility and express understanding
- Give benefit of doubt (legitimate reasons may exist)

**Pipeline Protection**
- Always maintain backup candidates
- Don't put all focus on one candidate
- Keep pipeline active until offer accepted
- Continue sourcing even with strong pipeline

**Root Cause Analysis**
- Identify patterns in drop-off (stage, role type, timeline)
- Get feedback on process from candidates who stay
- Improve communication and candidate experience
- Adjust timeline if too slow
- Review compensation competitiveness

# 4.7 Success Metrics & KPIs

## Individual Performance Metrics

### Speed Metrics
- **Average time-to-fill:** Target 25 days (vs 42-day industry average)
- **Time from posting to first submission:** Target 5 days
- **Time from submission to interview:** Target 3 days
- **Application response time:** Target <24 hours
- **Phone screen scheduling:** Target <48 hours

### Quality Metrics
- **Submission-to-interview ratio:** Target 3:1 or better
- **Interview-to-offer ratio:** Target 3:1 or better
- **Offer acceptance rate:** Target 85%+
- **90-day retention rate of hires:** Target 90%+
- **Hiring manager satisfaction:** Target 4.7/5
- **Candidate satisfaction (feedback surveys):** Target 4.5/5

### Volume Metrics
- **Number of positions filled per month:** Target 3-5 (depending on complexity)
- **Screens conducted per week:** Benchmark 20-30
- **Candidates submitted per role:** Average 8-10
- **Active pipeline size:** 50+ candidates per open role
- **New candidates sourced per week:** Target 25-40
- **Interviews coordinated per week:** Benchmark 10-15

### Efficiency Metrics
- **Percentage of time spent on value-add activities:** Target >60%
- **Sourcing response rate:** Target 10-15%
- **Application completion rate:** Target 85%+
- **Interview attendance rate:** Target 90%+

### Business Impact
- **Cost per hire:** Target 30% below market average
- **Source of hire diversity:** Balanced across channels
- **Passive candidate conversion rate:** Target 5-8%

- **Year-over-year improvement in key metrics:** Target 10% improvement

- **Daily active usage (logins per day):** Benchmark 10-15
